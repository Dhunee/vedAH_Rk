## Comment
Found also in Pāipp. xx. Used by Kāuç. (36. 38), in one of the rites concerning women, against a wife's lover, with a plant called bāṇāparṇī 'arrow-feather' (Dārila, ⌊çirapun̄khā: for which Bl. conjectures⌋ çarapun̄khā, which is Thephrosia purpurea
⌊Roth⌋, though T. spinosa is the spinous species).


## Translations
Translated: Weber, Ind. Stud. v. 250; Henry, 44, 122; Griffith, i. 382.
