[*3] At noon, after (the ceremonies) from the putting (of wood) on the fire down to the Agyabhaga oblations (have been performed), he puts a piece of Palasa wood on (the fire) with the next (verse; M. II, 7, 1), sits down to the west of the fire on a mat or on eraka grass, recites the next (verse, II, 7, 2) over a razor, and hands it over to the barber with the next Yagus (II, 7, 3). (The rites) beginning with the pouring together of (warm and cold) water down to the burying of the hair are the same as above (comp. M. II, 7, 4).
