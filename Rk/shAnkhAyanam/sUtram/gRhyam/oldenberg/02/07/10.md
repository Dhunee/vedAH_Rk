10 [^6] . 'Recite the Gāyatrī, sir!' says the other.


[^6]:  Comp. the note on chap. 5, 4-6. Nārāyaṇa states, in accordance with these Sūtras of the fifth chapter, that in case the student belongs to the second or third caste, an Ūha (i.e. a corresponding alteration of the formulas; from the Śrauta-sūtra, VI, 1, 3 the definition is quoted here śabdavikāram ūhaṃ bruvate) takes place. If he is a Kṣatriya, he has to say, 'Recite the Tṛṣṭubh, sir!''Recite the verse of Hiraṇyastūpa (Rig-veda I, 35, 2), sir!' A Vaiśya has to say, 'Recite the Jagatī, sir!''Recite the verse of Hiraṇyastūpa (or, of Vāmadeva, Rig-veda I, 35, 9 or IV, 40, 5), sir!'

'I recite the Gāyatrī to thee!' says the teacher.
