Earth
Atri Bhauma
3 verses: anuс╣гс╣нubh
This tiny hymn is attributed to the same poet as the preceding hymn to Parjanya and complements that hymn. The poem is a sort of riddle: the first verse establishes that Earth is the addressee, and in the next verse the address to a feminine being (the word тАЬearthтАЭ is feminine in gender and conceived of as female) continues. But none of the characteristics mentioned in that verse seem particularly Earth-like. She is addressed as oscillating and silvery; she is associated with the nights; she тАЬflingsтАЭ moisture. The riddle isтАФunder what circumstances could Earth be so described? The answer: under the darkness of the monsoon clouds, when thunder causes her to shake (thus тАЬoscillatingтАЭ; see V.83.5) and she is тАЬsilveryтАЭ with rain, which she тАЬflingsтАЭ in the forms of streams and rivulets down her slopes, mentioned in verse 1. This solution was adumbrated by Thieme (1964: 58) and is discussed further in Jamison (2013).
If the audience doesnтАЩt solve the riddle in verse 2, verse 3 provides the answer.
