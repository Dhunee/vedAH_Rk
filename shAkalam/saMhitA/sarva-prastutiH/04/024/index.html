The big question about this vs. is the identity of the speaker. The standard view is that it is the poet V─Бmadeva, who is putting Indra on sale temporarily, with the requirement that he be returned after his obstacle-smashing is done. I find this unlikely. How did Indra come to be possessed by V─Бmadeva (im├бm тАж m├бm├йndram тАЬthis Indra of mineтАЭ)? Who is he hawking Indra to? Why has the scene changed from the domestic one of Indra and his wife to, presumably, the ritual ground? My own suggestion, albeit somewhat tentative, is that the speaker is IndraтАЩs wife. Who would have a better right to call him тАЬthis Indra of mineтАЭ? Moreover, there seems no good reason to introduce his wife as an emphatic actor in vs. 8 and then drop her out of the story.
