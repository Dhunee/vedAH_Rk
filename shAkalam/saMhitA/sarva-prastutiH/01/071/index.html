+Jamison Brereton Notes

A difficult verse with a discouragingly large number of competing interpretations. In outline, though not in detail, I follow GeldnerтАЩs interpretation: p─Бda a concerns the production of poetry and the poetic vision by our side, in contrast to the poetic visions of the stranger, our rival, in b. After the strangerтАЩs attempts are gotten rid of, the topic returns in cd to our poetic products, which have success. (Witzel Got┼Н basically follow Geldner, while Oldenberg [SBE] and Renou take b as coreferential with cd.) The first hemistich, esp. its first p─Бda, is highly alliterative and rhyming: d├бdhann с╣Ыt├бс╣Г dhan├бyann asya dh─лt├нm, ─Б╠Бd ├нd ary├│ didhiс╣гv├▓ v├нbhс╣Ыt─Бс╕е.
